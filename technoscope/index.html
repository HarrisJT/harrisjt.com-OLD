Project

Technoscope

Technoscope was my first foray into blogging and creating themes for the Ghost platform. I loved working with handlebars and look forward to restarting my blog on this platform. Posts featured my personal writing on various tech languages as I was learning them as well as writing about originality, creativity, and productivity. I wanted to also occasionally post my thoughts on books, podcasts, articles, or software in a separate category to show interest and benefit myself through SEO. Unfortunately, I stopped maintaining this blog so the posts are gone but I could spin up the design on a placeholder server for screenshots.

User Experience & Sitemap

I split the website up into four tags and two utility pages. The homepage was a limited number of post previews for the latest articles and a read more button to open them. This mimics the common blog style so that users can move around the page as easily as possible. Development, Design, Mind, and Review are the tag categories of writing and each page has a preview of the latest articles for each relevant tag. These pages have pagination instead of infinite scrolling so as to not overwhelm the viewer. The Development and Design pages’ feature writings on web technologies and languages that I use as well as best practice and beginner’s principles. The Mind page featured writings on productivity, originality, creativity, and the learning process. The Review page includes writings about books I read, podcasts I enjoy, other featured blogs or people in the industry to show how interested I am in certain topics and give my opinions.

Typography

Fonts Choice

The heading font that I chose is Lato because it is very legible and contrasts the serif font I used in the body. I chose Roboto Slab, the popular Google Android font derivative, because it is a very modern serif font that balances against the heading font. In this website, the font is very important because the user will spend most of their time reading. I wanted a popular font in serif because at the time research showed that serif was easier to read for body content.

Color

Colors Choice

I am most proud of the colors for this website. I am happy with the palate and really do want to put it to use again. The header is a dark blue font that makes the white and pink logo pop as well as the brightly colored navigation hover styles. Each of the navigation options has a brightly colored line above it that blends into the button when hovered on. The main content rests in a nearly white wrapper against a slightly darker gray. The box shadow on the content makes this look like layered and gives the design a refreshing depth. I have since then employed the technique of using dark and light colors with multiple brightly colored accents in the latest two version of my portfolios.
