Project

DeepTunnelMKE

The Deep Tunnel of Milwaukee is one of Wisconsin's greatest feats of engineering and has been nothing short of success in its operation. The tunnel is an advanced urban rain and sewage overflow prevention system that stores excess runoff until it can be treated.
This website was created as a school project and turned out to be a fantastic learning experience. The goal of this project has been to create an appealing design that would contrast the government and journalist resources for information on the Deep Tunnel. The research aims to dispel the claims of incompetency as well as provide information on the history and context of the complex tunnel system.

Analysis

User Experience & Sitemap

When creating the sitemap, I took the ideas for our webpages and grouped them into two categories of Research & Impact and History because I didn't like how competing sites were splitting up similar information across many pages. Creating the navigation was satisfying because the final design integrates with the overall website design and remains usable. The menus also have the feature of not disappearing immediately after the mouse moves off them. Small interactions such as the hover styles, active styles, animated illustration and the rotating dropdown menu indicators add up to a user’s experience and make a large impact.

Typography

Fonts Choice

I wanted the website to be very easy to read because it is primarily for research and providing information. I chose the body font of Open Sans because it is very neutral and legible with a wide amount of font weights easily available. I often use this font for body text, including in this portfolio. I chose the font Dosis Medium because I felt that it made more sense with the “friendly” design of the overall webpage. I had previously been using Montserrat but the whole aspect of the design shifted to “simple” and so did the font.

Color

Colors Choice

The overall design of the website is fun and friendly contrasting the competitors dull experience so the colors chosen had to reflect that. I wanted the content to rest on a light background but to avoid wasted space I centered the light gray content background on the dark blue website background. This design decision also has the benefit of focusing the users’ attention to the centered content. The orange accent color plays off the gray and dark blue to be noticeable but not clashing.
